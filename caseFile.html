Company A developed a video game EVO in which the player controls a fish trying to survive in an aquatic environment. In order to survive (and gain points) the fish must avoid bigger fish and eat smaller fish. As the player gains points the player controlled fish grows in size allowing for it to eat previous enemies. The player completes a level by eating all competition on screen. Company A files for Trademark for the game title and character designs. Company A also filed for copyright of the games assets (music, game design, character sprites). Company A does not intent to make a series out of EVO.

Company B developed a video game called EVOLUTION. In this game the player controls an evolving avatar that takes on traits of other creatures it eats. The majority of the game the avatar is that of a fish, with extra bits and appendages added to it. The goal of the game is to get to the end of the level. While possible to complete without evolving, the evolution mechanic is key to winning. Company B files for both Copyright and Trademark the game. Company B intents to make EVOLUTION a series.
